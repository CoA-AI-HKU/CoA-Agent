## Day Care Centre Activities – Companion Dog Service

Recently, a “Companion Dog Service” visit was successfully held at our centre, sponsored by the Andy Lau Charity Foundation Limited and organized by Hong Kong Seeing Eye Dog Services. During the event, handler Eric brought along the gentle and friendly companion dog Joyce to interact with our residents, creating a warm and joyful atmosphere.

Under Eric’s guidance, Joyce engaged closely with the residents, bringing smiles to many service users faces. The scene was heartwarming and lively. We sincerely thank handler Eric and companion dog Joyce for their visit—their professionalism, patience, and gentle companionship provided a loving and joyful experience for the users.

## Day Care Centre Activities – Magic Show

Our centre recently welcomed volunteers Vincent from the Hong Kong Charity Volunteer Team, who brought a spectacular magic performance and singing show for our residents. The atmosphere was filled with laughter and warmth.

Vincent impressed everyone with their amazing magic tricks and beautiful singing. The interactive segments added unexpected joy for the service users, creating moments of surprise and delight. Their lively performance not only entertained but also conveyed care and warmth, giving every participant a memorable and happy time.

We sincerely thank the Hong Kong Charity Volunteer Team and both performers for their selfless dedication—their enthusiasm and professionalism brought joy and positive energy to our residents.

## Day Care Centre Activity - Egg Tart Making Workshop

To promote cognitive training and social interaction among service users, we recently held an “Egg Tart Making Workshop.” This activity aimed to provide multi-sensory stimulation and opportunities for social engagement through the nostalgic experience of making traditional pastries.

During the workshop, our services users not only revisited the craft of making traditional treats but also exercised their fine motor skills through hands-on practice. In the tasting session, everyone enjoyed their handmade egg tarts while sharing fond memories, creating a warm and cheerful atmosphere filled with laughter.

Our centre regularly organizes various cognitive and social activities. Members of the public are welcome to book a visit.

## New Season of Courses on the EDU Platform Launches

In June 2023, the Centre launched Hong Kong’s first comprehensive online learning platform for dementia: EDU. This platform offers various courses that share knowledge and care skills related to dementia through videos and interactive exercises in an engaging and accessible manner. Users can explore dementia knowledge flexibly anytime and anywhere. Each course provides notes to help users grasp key points and review care tips.

The platform has now launched a new season of course content, offering “Fall Prevention.” Interested individuals can learn more and enroll in the courses at：[https://edu.jccpa.org.hk/course/](https://edu.jccpa.org.hk/course/)
