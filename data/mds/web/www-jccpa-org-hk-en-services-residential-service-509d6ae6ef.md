# Residential Service

## Flexible and Autonomous Relieving Caregivers Stress

###### A team of professional dementia care specialists including nurses, physiotherapists, and social workers to provide round-the-clock personalized care based on the residents’ conditions and needs. They also offer one-stop care and training services to help slow down the residents’ decline and reduce the caregivers’ stress, striving to create a safe and cozy “home” for them.

######

###### We adhere to the “people-centered care” philosophy in caring for each people with dementia.

###### We respect every people with dementia, recognizing their ability to enjoy an autonomous life without restraints.

###### We hope that every people with dementia can live independently and age gracefully.

## Six Key Features

01

###### Professional case management:
Case managers discuss and formulate care plans with the team

02

###### People-centered care model

03

###### Respect for residents:
No use of restraints

04

###### Dementia-friendly environment

05

###### Provision of short-term* or long-term accommodation

06

###### Each bed is equipped with multiple bed-exit sensing systems

*Short-term residency is available only to members using the Day Care Service or those who have completed service in the Jockey Club Post Discharge Programme for People with Dementia.

## Targeted Service Users

People with dementia who have residential needs and meet the following conditions:

1. Do not have any serious illnesses, such as recurrent heart disease, or dependencies, such as oxygen therapy, and are not bed-ridden.
2. Do not have extensive nursing requirements, such as Peritoneal Dialysis or nasal feeding.
3. Are able to eat by mouth and swallow safely – no tube feeding is needed.
4. Only need one person to help lift or move about.
5. Do not create a serious disturbance or display aggressive behaviours.
