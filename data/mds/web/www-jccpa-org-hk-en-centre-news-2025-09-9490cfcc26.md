## Day Care Centre Activity – Aromatherapy Session

Recently, the Centre was honored to welcome volunteers from Incandescent Healing Limited, who brought a warm and beneficial aromatherapy session to the day care users.

During the event, participants enjoyed relaxing scents and experienced the multiple benefits of aromatherapy. Seeing the joyful smiles on the elderly participants’ faces was the greatest reward.

Heartfelt thanks to Incandescent Healing Limited for organizing this meaningful morning filled with fragrance and laughter.

## Mid-Autumn Festival Celebration

To celebrate the Mid-Autumn Festival, the Centre organized festive activities for both day care and residential service users. Participants shared folk stories and customs, and made mooncakes together.

In addition to traditional snowy mooncakes, soft-textured mooncakes were also prepared to cater to those with swallowing difficulties, ensuring everyone could enjoy the festive treats.

These interactive sessions not only evoked memories and raised awareness of the festival (a form of reminiscence therapy), but also helped train cognitive skills, hand-eye coordination, and spatial awareness.

## JCCPA 25th Anniversary Presents: Echoes of Memory Exhibition

To mark its 25th anniversary, the Centre is hosting the “Echoes of Memory Exhibition” from October 1 to 10, 2025, at D·PARK, Tsuen Wan.

This 10-day interactive exhibition aims to promote public awareness of brain health and dementia prevention through engaging and educational activities.

The opening ceremony was graced by notable guests, including:

- Chris Sun, JP (Secretary for Labour and Welfare),
- The Honourable Bernard Charnwut CHAN, GBS, JP (Steward, The Hong Kong Jockey Club),
- Lam Ching-choi, GBS, JP (Chairman, Advisory Committee on Mental Health and Chief Executive Officer, Haven of Hope Christian Service),
- Philip CHIU, JP (Dean, Faculty of Medicine, CUHK),
- Simon Tang (Cluster Chief Executive of New Territories East Cluster), and
- Ali Lee (JCCPA Ambassador).

All are welcome to visit and help build a dementia-friendly community.

## Training Courses for Dementia Care

With the generous support from the Hong Kong Jockey Club Community Project Grant, JCCPA continues to organise the training programme for caregivers.

## New Season of Courses on the EDU Platform Launches

In June 2023, the Centre launched Hong Kong’s first comprehensive online learning platform for dementia: EDU. This platform offers various courses that share knowledge and care skills related to dementia through videos and interactive exercises in an engaging and accessible manner. Users can explore dementia knowledge flexibly anytime and anywhere. Each course provides notes to help users grasp key points and review care tips.

The platform has now launched a new season of course content, offering “Daily Life Care.” Interested individuals can learn more and enroll in the courses at：[https://edu.jccpa.org.hk/course/](https://edu.jccpa.org.hk/course/)
