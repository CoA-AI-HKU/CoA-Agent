# 2023-07

## Training Courses for Dementia Care

With the support from the Hong Kong Jockey Club Community Project Grant, JCCPA has launched a new programme “Training Courses for Dementia Care”.

The two intensive training courses were successfully held on 15 July. Ms. Ally Lam, the registered social worker, has shared with over 150 caregivers regarding the symptoms of dementia, communication skills, effective caregiving tactics on taking care people with dementia in different stages and care plan etc. During the talk, she also offered practical caring tips to beginners in order to take care people with dementia more effectively.

The next training course is scheduled on 19 Aug (Sat) 10am. Dr Chan Wai Chi will share with us regarding psychiatric medication. For more details and registration, please click the link: [https://www.jccpa.org.hk/s/2023-S3-CG-training](https://www.jccpa.org.hk/s/2023-S3-CG-training)

## "Jockey Club Post-diagnostic Support in Dementia Care Programme" was honored at "The 11th Asia Pacific Eldercare Innovation Awards 2023"

“The 11th Asia Pacific Eldercare Innovation Awards 2023” is hosted by Singapore’s Ageing Asia and is known as the “Oscars of the elderly care industry”.

An award ceremony was held locally recently, and we were awarded as one of the finalists of the “Best Dementia Care Programme” for its “Jockey Club Post-diagnostic Support in Dementia Care Programme”, demonstrating our innovative and outstanding contribution to supporting people with dementia. , which also proves the industry’s recognition of us.

We are deeply honored to receive this honor, which marks recognition of our team’s unremitting efforts and persistent pursuit, and once again creates another milestone for us; at the same time, we are grateful to all our partners and clients who support us. Your support and trust allow us to continue to innovate and provide better care and support for people with dementia. We will continue to work hard to promote innovative projects, establish models of high-quality dementia services, and enable people with dementia to age at home in the community.
