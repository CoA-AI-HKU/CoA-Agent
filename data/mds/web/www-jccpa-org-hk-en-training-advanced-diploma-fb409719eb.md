# Advanced Diploma Programme in Dementia Care

###### Organised by the Department of Medicine and Therapeutics, Faculty of Medicine, The Chinese University of Hong Kong, and co-organised by JCCPA, this programme helps professional workers and people interested in dementia care understand dementia from different perspectives and learn about the practical application of cross-disciplinary professional intervention and care for dementia.

## Programme Objectives:

- to address clinical issues in dementia care and service delivery
- to enhance collaborative efforts and holistic care by the multidisciplinary intervention
- to strengthen the ethics and clinical assessments in dementia care
- to analyse existing research, approaches and applications in the local context
- to develop knowledge and skills in social services evaluation related to dementia care

## Programme Duration

1 year (2 semesters), September 2026 to April 2027

## Targets

Professional medical and nursing workers providing elderly care services (including social workers, nurses, occupational therapists and physiotherapists), and care professionals interested in a career in dementia care

## Sharing from Alumni

The course made me realised the crucial importance of person-centred approach to dementia care. The knowledge and skills helped me to develop new awareness and ability. I am feeling reassured, supported and be confident to provide effective strategies while engaging vulnerable or complex care for the people with dementia.

This course facilitates me to attain professionalism and enhance my comprehensive understanding towards dementia, people with dementia and the caregivers. Inspired by this course, I learnt to adopt a more person-centred approach. Understanding is just enough but being empathetic will surely make a difference.

The course enhanced my knowledge and spectrum of management in dementia. Through multidisciplinary model and understanding about person-centred care approach, I am equipped with the capability to formulate a management plan for people with dementia in an effi cient way. The arrangement of different clinical visits further broadened my view on the implementation of person-centred care model for clients with different stages and types of dementia.
